Push Rod Rear Suspension Design

This Project was completed as a suspension subteam member at the UBC Formula Electric Design Team.

Project Goal: Redesign the rear suspension to allow for the addition of in-hub motors onto the car.

Responsibility: I was tasked with managing this project, involving determening the layout and pakcaging of the suspension system, design all components and manufacturing components

Final Design: Shown in the above figure is the rear suspension on our 2024 formula car at competition. The design is a push-rod suspension design encorporating a bell crank, shock, push rod and an anti roll bar (ARB).

Actions: Listed below are all the tasks I completed for this Project

  • Analyzed 2023 formula car rear suspension system, understanding suspension dynamics using Microsoft Excel and SolidWorks.
  • Analyzed FSAE Michigan Competition rules, specifically relating to suspension components and suspension design.
  • Presented multiple rear suspension design configurations and componenet packagin at mechanical team design review, final packaging shown in figure 1.
  • Designed rear suspension layout and geometry in SolidWorks.
  • Designed first iteration of rear bell crank in SolidWorks (figure 2).
  • Performed SolidWorks FEA simulation and topology study on first iteration of bell crank (figure 3) to minimize bell crank weight and ensure the bell cranks meet stress requirements.
  • Designed manufacturing jig for rear suspension chassis mounts (figure 4).
  • Designed Bell crank, shock and ARB chassis mounting brackets in SolidWorks.
  • Performed SolidWorks FEA simulation on bell crank and shock chassis mounting brackets to ensure the parts meet stress requirements.
  • Manufactured chassis mounting brackets using water jet cutter.
  • Assembled first iteration of bell cranks using general shop tools.

Result: The redesigned rear suspension system was used on our 2024 competition car, competing at the FSAE Michigan Competition. The design passed all mechanical inspection and performed succesfully.
