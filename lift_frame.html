Manage Pressure Drilling Lifting Frame

Project completed when working as an engineering work term student at Frontier Inc.

For confidentiality reasons, I cannot provide any images, drawings, client names or project locations for work completed during this project and can only show select SolidWorks model screenshots.

Project Goal: Design a lifting frame to lift a 2-meter tall, 1 meter diameter, 7 tonne Manage Pressure Drilling (MPD) unit onto the Blowout Preventer (BOP) that is located on the drilling floor of an offshore oil production facility.

Responsibility: I was tasked with completing all design and SolidWorks modelling for this project.

Final Design: Shown in the above is the final assembly containing the MPD unit in grey and the designed lifting frame in yellow.

Actions: Listed below are all the tasks I completed for this project:

  • Analyzed engineering drawings and provided 3D model of MPD unit using SolidWorks and AutoCAD.
  • Analyzed the drilling floor and Blowout Preventer of the offshore oil production facility in Navisworks to identify lifting frame interface points and overall environment layout.
  • Created a SolidWorks model for the MPD unit lift frame
  • Developed engineering drawings of the MPD unit lift frame for manufacturing
  • Created presentations for client meetings and presented the concept and project progress at various stages of the project.
  • Collaborated with structural engineers to review Finite Element Analysis (FEA) data to ensure lifting frame met offshore structural lifting standards.

Result: The lifting frame was manufactured based on the SolidWorks model and the manufacturing drawings I created. The MPD unit was successfully installed onto the BOP of the offshore oil platform in early 2024.
